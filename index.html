1589

Queen Elizabeth Said

"Consider thou what the invention could do to my poor sub-jects. It would assuredly bring to them ruin by depriving them of employment, thus making them beggars."

1700

Britain Takes the Lead

While other countries had strict regulations, Britain's lassie-faire approach to technology allowed them to take an early lead in the industrial revolution.

1793

Cotton Gin - Structural Unemployment

When the Cotton Gin was invented, thousands of Cotton workers became unemployed. This unemployment is called structural unemployment - when workers just do not have the skills for the job. In the long-run, structural unemployment goes away.

1927

The Great Depression

In the leadup to the great depression, unemployment among rural workers had begun to increase as technologies like tractors started to automate the farming process.

2018

Fourth Industrial Revolution

As technological development explodes, people begin to become more and more worried about how robots and computers will affect jobs. However, GDP has only grown - yet, income inequality continues to suffer.

2030

Projections

Coming technologies like self-driving cars and mechine learning are only expected to increase worries about unemployment, but no mainstream economists predict this.

2100

Singularity

Prof. Vinge proposes that Artificial Intelligence will get so powerful that humans will no longer need to work. This is an idea called the singularity. Theoriticans are skeptical that this could happen.
