350 BC

Aristotle Wrote

"if every instrument could accomplish its own work, obeying or anticipating the will of others...chief workmen would not want servants, nor masters slaves." Here Aristotle proclaims on of the first worries of mass unemployment.

1589

Queen Elizabeth Said

"Consider thou what the invention could do to my poor sub-jects. It would assuredly bring to them ruin by depriving them of employment, thus making them beggars." Even back in the 1500's people worried about technology causing unemployment.

1793

Cotton Gin - Structural Unemployment

When the Cotton Gin was invented, thousands of Cotton workers became unemployed. This unemployment is called structural unemployment - when workers just do not have the skills for the job. In the long-run, structural unemployment goes away as seen by the cotton gin not causing long run unemployment.

1800

Britain Takes the Lead

While other countries had strict regulations, Britain's lassie-faire approach to technology allowed them to take an early lead in the industrial revolution. This is an example of a countries lack of worries about technology bringing them ultimate success.

1927

The Great Depression

In the leadup to the great depression, unemployment among rural workers had begun to increase as technologies like tractors started to automate the farming process. However, in the long run this unemployment went away, and farm technology only bettered the world.

2018

Fourth Industrial Revolution

As technological development explodes, people begin to become more and more worried about how robots and computers will affect jobs. However, technology companies have only helped grow GDP and raise global welfare. Yet, income inequality has grown as production power goes to the top. Policy needs to be put in place to prevent this.

2030

Projections

Coming technologies like self-driving cars and machine learning are only expected to increase worries about unemployment. However, mainstream economists only predict that they will better people on average. Technology jobs will become more common, and people will work less in manual labor and such.

2100

Singularity

Prof. Vinge proposes that Artificial Intelligence will get so powerful that humans will no longer need to work. This is an idea called the singularity. Theoriticans are skeptical that this could happen. However, in the case that it does, public policy needs to be ready to adapt.
